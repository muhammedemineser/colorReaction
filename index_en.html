Good to know
What happens in your body when you react?
Your eyes (receptors) detect colors and send the information to the brain via afferent nerves. There the brain decides what to do. The signal then travels back through efferent pathways to your fingers – you type! The brain checks via reafference whether everything was carried out as planned.
This interplay of perception and action is called sensorimotor function.

Game hints
How fast is your brain really?
When you see a color in the game and react to it, a whole lot happens inside your body — and you hardly notice!

1. Perceive the stimulus (approx. 50–100 ms)
Your eyes detect the color. The cells in the retina send the info via afferent nerves to the brain.

2. Process & recognize the color (approx. 100–150 ms)
Your brain says: “Ah – that's red!” and compares it with your memory.

3. Make a decision (approx. 70–100 ms)
You quickly choose the right action plan: “I'll write red!”

4. Execute the action (approx. 100–150 ms)
Your command travels through efferent pathways to your fingers — you start typing!

The average human needs about 1,000ms for all of this—test yourself! How good are you?
